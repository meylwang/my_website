VSCO
Journals

Young adults love to share experiences with others through photography. VSCO is a mobile app that helps them take, edit, and share photos with a wide audience, all in one place. The "Journal" is a collection of photos with captioning and text in a blog style that allows users to share a more cohesive story.
‍
I redesigned the experience of journal creation to allow for more freedom of creation and discoverability of Journal capabilities.

Task Analysis

I initially tested three main features of VSCO with two new users

1) Publishing the user’s own, edited photo
2) Accessing the user’s best friend’s photo on their profile and sharing it
3) Creating and sharing a journal (including image captions).

While they experienced minor confusion with tasks 1 and 2, the most confusion occurred in task 3. Users did not know what "Journal" meant in the context of VSCO, and once in the journal creation process did not discover captioning and multiple photo add functionality.

I then created flows and prototypes for two ways to create a journal.

Direction 2

Ideation, Storyboarding, and Paper Prototypes
Mid-fi prototype and heuristic evaluation

After conducting a brief Heuristic Evaluation, I made two major edits.

1. Joining the canvas creation of Direction 2 with the photo vs. journal distinction of Direction 1

2. Adding fading out as guides to where users could drag images and text

3. Unclear slide to edit functionality was changed to a simple tap to see the edit and delete buttons

These resulted in the medium fidelity prototype displayed below.

Heuristic Evaluation and Mid-fi Prototype

I then had users complete the task
"Share a collection of captioned photos with your VSCO followers"
I noticed that even with a wiggling animation on tap, users did not realize they could drag the photo and text buttons. Users also wanted the option of publishing journals privately.

I incorporated this input by switching the animation to a movement down to the canvas and then fade (to mimic the act of dragging and dropping) and by adding a toggle between private and public on the cover photo page.
