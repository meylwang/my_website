Charity

Many fresh college graduates find it extremely difficult to discover charities they consider legitimate and trustworthy enough to donate small amounts to. Charity Navigator is a site and mobile app pairing that ranks charities on an objective scale, allowing users to make more informed decisions. The mobile app currently suffers from an unclear sorting mechanism, information overload and an unclear presentation of the rating system.

comparative research

For initial research, I identified our target audience (first time users and fresh graduates) and designed an interview guide to test three charity-giving apps (Cauze, One Today, and Charity Navigator). In order to investigate users' process of finding a charity to donate to, we designed the hypothetical scenario...

“Your friend’s birthday is coming up and instead of giving them a birthday present they want you to donate to a charitable cause of your choosing. They suggested using these apps for you to find a charity you would be interested in.”

Our team interviewed a total of 8 fresh graduates, each interview lasting for an hour.

Collection of nitpicks and needs extracted from the initial interviews



Synthesizing the content from interviews I identified 5 main insights:

1.
People want to find charities that apply to a topic they care about or that have a specific area of impact.
2.
A lack of information makes users mistrustful and too much information causes users to fatigue.
3.
Users want to understand the tangible impact of their donations.
4.
People tend to be more likely to donate if they know that others have donated to the same charity
5.
Images and rating systems can create as much distrust as trust if not executed properly
Initial Ideation

I chose to redesign Charity Navigator because, out of the 3 apps, it provided the best content, only lacking a good UX for discovering the content. The key questions I kept in mind while brainstorming were...

Discovery: How do users find charities they care about?
Impact: How do we convey impact per dollar?
Credibility: What information should we display to help users evaluate charities?

Concept 1: Venmo like charity giving system increases ease of donation
Concept 2: Virtual tree growing creates an interactive and social experience around donating

The wireflow I ended up pursuing  (above) focused on establishing credibility of the charity. It uses a video, Charity Navigator’s rating system, an impact statement, reviews, and metrics to achieve credibility.

Ethical Design

As this project hypothetically involves non-profit organizations, the ethical considerations that arose as we discussed our different wireframes were especially pertinent. Some of the issues that we found relevant to highlight for teams continuing research in this direction are...

1. Displaying average donation size is a good way to establish credibility but may alienate people who can’t afford to donate large amounts. We adopted a histogram within our redesign to allow the average donation to appear as a range rather than a number.

2. Users are influenced by reputation, better designed mission statements, and publicity content to prefer established charities over new ones. It is important to help newer charities get discovered as well, so we tested out various ways to balance the discovery of new and established charities.

3. It seems pointless to display charities with lower ratings due to the rarity of receiving a donation. However, the function of Charity Navigator is to first educate through being objective and transparent, so we continued to display lower rated charities to facilitate informed decision making.

Conclusion & Future

The final app design takes steps to address the user needs identified in the initial needfinding interviews I conducted. Over several rounds of RITE testing, I learned what patterns, flows, and visual design features were effective. Each element on the charity information page was questioned and modified to prevent information or overload. The work done in this app redesign will help users find charities they care about, but is not finished. Some edits to implement in the future include...

1. The map organization by area-of-impact needs further testing as it was implemented later during RITE testing

2. The call to action needs to be stronger as the act of donating is underemphasized in our current design

3. The Explore Page and Bookmarking functionality were not the focus of the redesign and can be more thoroughly fleshed out
